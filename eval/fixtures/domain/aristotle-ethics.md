# Nicomachean Ethics — Aristotle

Aristotle defines happiness (eudaimonia) as activity of the soul in accordance with virtue over a complete life.
The mean between excess and deficiency is found by practical wisdom (phronesis) in each situation.
Friendship of virtue is rare and lasting; pleasure and utility friendships are inferior forms.

Moral virtue is acquired by habit: we become just by doing just actions, not by reading definitions alone.
